The fort Sindhudurg is on an island off the coast of Makin town in the Sindhudurg district of the Konkan region of Maharashtra State. This fort is a protected monument by the Central Government of India.

➩ Districts/Region
Malvan Taluka, Sindhudurg district, Maharashtra, India.

➩ Geography
Sindhudurg Fort is located on an island with the Arabian Sea in the West, the Malvan Coast in the East about 1.5 KM away.

History

The shape of Malvan beach is a crescent with protruding rocks in the sea. This can be seen as the unique geological feature of this beach. Sindhudurga was built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. The fort is on a moderate rock that is not far from the Malvan coast. The fort is known for being the coastal capital of the Maratha Empire and a major Naval base during the time of Shivaji Maharaj. The rampart walls of the fort arising from the sea is a unique and surprising feature here. The height and width of the fort and in its main temple, Shivaji Maharaj's image is worshipped. The hand and feet prints of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj are kept at the walls offer grandeur to the fort structure. The entrance gate of this fort is in the North-East direction. It is believed that this fort was once full of buildings, but now it is merely a shell with nothing inside except a few small temples. To the Marathas, Sindhudurg is Shivaji's cenotaph, temple in reverence and protected by small domes. The fort has few water tanks which provide drinking water. Realising the importance of the navy in the Arabian sea and coastal security, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj wanted to have a strong fort in Konkan Area. After various unsuccessful attempts to conquer Janjira of Siddhis near Murud, he planned for this sea jewel, known as Sindhudurga around 1665. Apart from the main fort of Sindhudurgs on the larger island, Shivaji Maharaj fortified the smaller island with a small fort Padmagad to protect the main fort. On the mainland opposite the town and at the mouth of the creek about a mile and a half north, built the forts of Rajkotand Sarjekot.

The temperature reaches up to 30 degrees Celsius during this season. Summers are hot and humid, and the temperature touches 40 degrees Celsius.
